This is my Pho Recipe

This gon be lit!

Equipment list

  • 16-25 quart stock pot
  • 10in sauce pan
  • Direct flame contact such as a butane portable stove or grill. Broil setting on an oven will do
  • Ladle
  • Large bowl for washing ingredients
  • Noodle strainer or colander
  • Cheese cloth and kitchen twine
  • Knife and cutting board

Ingredients List

Broth

    Makes about 12 quarts of broth

  • 4 onions
  • 6 fingers of ginger
  • 8 large shallots
  • 2 bunches of green onions
  • 3 bunches of coriander
  • 5lbs of beef knee and neckbones
  • 2lbs of trimmed beef brisket
  • 1lb of beef shank
  • 1-2lbs beef tendon
  • 1lb beef tripe, cleaned
  • 1lb of beef meatballs
  • 3-4 medium lumps of rock sugar
  • 1 whole black cardamom pod
  • 7 cloves
  • 2 tablspoons of black pepper
  • 1 stick of cinnamon or cassia bark (vietnamese cinnamon)
  • 1 cup of fish sauce
  • Salt to taste

Serving suggestion

  • Pho noodles (Banh pho)
  • Coriander (cilantro)
  • Thai/holy basil
  • blanched mungbean sprouts
  • lime
  • Sliced white onion
  • Sliced brisket
  • Sliced beef tendon
  • Sliced beef tripe
  • Raw sliced beef tenderloin
  • Fish sauce
  • Ground black pepper
  • Thai chilis
  • Sriracha
  • Hoisin sauce

Make the Broth

The broth is the key to any good serving of pho.

  1. Bring about 9 quarts of water to a rolling boil
  2. While water is boiling, take the spices and toast them on low in a sauce pan. Allow spices to become fragrant.
  3. Remove spices from the pan and let cool. Make a satchet using the cheesecloth and tie shut using kitchen twine.
  4. While water is boiling, wash the onions, ginger, and shallots. Pat dry and then char over your heat source until you see char marks. Let cool.
  5. When the water is boiling, add in the bones, brisket, tendons, and shanks. Boil for about 6-8 minutes
  6. Remove the bones and protein from the stockpot and set aside in a bowl of clean water
  7. Once boiled, empty the stockpot of the water used to boil the ingredients. Clean the pot of all protein and then bring 12 quarts of water to a boil.
  8. While water is boiling, clean the bones and protein under cold running water to remove impurities.
  9. Return bones and protein to the stockpot
  10. Add in the charred aromatics, green onions, and spice satchel to the pot
  11. Simmer on medium-low heat for about 3-4 hours, skimming the top of the broth for impurities as they surface
  12. At about 2.5 hours, add in 3/4 of the fish sauce and rock sugar. Taste and adjust to your preferences.
  13. At the 3 hours mark, add in the corriander
  14. Taste and adjust. The broth should be on the stronger/saltier side as the noodles will make the serving a bit more bland
  15. Remove from stove and allow to sit overnight for the flavor to develop
